Ruby-throated Hummingbird

Archilocus colubris, or the ruby-throated hummingbird, at just 3 inches long, is the smallest bird in Minnesota and can start to be seen around the state in the first weeks of May.

Bumblebee

Bombus terricola, or the yellow-banded bumblebee, have adapted to cold weather by having thick, long hair and are often found in colder climates.

Monarch Butterfly

Danaus plexippus, or the monarch butterfly, feeds on milkweed and flowering plants and migrates by the millions from Minnesota to the same location in Mexico each fall to spend the winter months.

Bottle gentian

Gentiana andrewsii, or bottle gentian, are popular with bumblebees because they are the only insects strong enough to pry open the closed flowers.

Blue false indigo

Baptisia australis, or blue false indigo, takes two full years to reach maturity and is a huge attractant for pollinators.

Harebell

Campanula rotundifolia, or harebell, gets its name from the folk belief that witches used this flower to transform into hares.

Maidenhair fern

Adiantum pedatum, or the maidenhair fern, is a popular houseplant and also resistant to damage from deer outside.

Virginia bluebells

Mertensia virginica, or virginia bluebells, bloom soon after the snow melts and go completely dormant in the winter.

Coreopsis Palmata

Coreopsis palmata, or stiff coreopsis, is also known as tickseed and blooms for 3-4 weeks during the midsummer.
